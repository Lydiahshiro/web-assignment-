General anaesthesia

General anaesthesia rapid sequence induction

Used for elective or emergency surgery in patients who are either not fasted or where there is a risk of aspiration of gastric contents. The airway is protected from aspiration by the application of cricoid pressure prior to rapid intubation using a depolarizing muscle relaxant. Thereafter, the anaesthetic technique is the same as an ETT with IPPV in the previous section.

Basic principles in ASA 1/2 patient:

  • Pre-oxygenate the patient for 3 minutes
  • Attach basic monitoring (SpO2, ECG and NIBP)
  • Proper preparation
  • tipping trolley
  • Equipment checked
  • suction available

Induction

  1. Intravenous anaesthetics.
    (thiopental or propofol)
  2. Suxamethonium to facilitate intubation with cricoid pressure applied by anaesthetic assistant to prevent aspiration
  3. Oral intubation and confirmation of tube placement by visualization, auscultation and capnography
  4. Release of cricoid pressure
  5. Non-depolarizing muscle relaxant to maintain muscle relaxation

Maintenance

  1. Oxygen/air or oxygen/nitrous oxide plus volatile agent
  2. Top up doses of muscle relaxant guided by neuromuscular monitoring additional analgesia as required

Emergence

  1. 100% oxygen
  2. Turn off volatile or intravenous agent
  3. Reverse neuromuscular block

Remove ETT when patient spontaneously breathing, regains airway reflexes and wakes up. It is recommended that this is performed with the patient in the left lateral position.
